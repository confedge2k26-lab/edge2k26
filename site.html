About EDGE 2026

International Conference · 15th–16th December 2026

Emerging Digital Intelligence and Generative Engineering (EDGE 2026) focuses on the transformative evolution of artificial intelligence, computational intelligence, and generative systems that are now shaping the future of automation, decision-making, knowledge representation, and digital ecosystems. The conference aims to establish an intellectual platform that brings together academic researchers, industry innovators, policymakers, and practitioners to explore theoretical advancements and practical implementations of emerging intelligent technologies. The theme reflects a growing global movement toward next-generation intelligent solutions that are capable of understanding, generating, reasoning, adapting, and interacting with humans and digital ecosystems in more meaningful ways. As digital systems become increasingly autonomous, generative, and interconnected, the conference positions itself as a catalyst for responsible innovation and interdisciplinary research.

Scope of the Conference

The scope of EDGE 2026 encompasses foundational theories, applied research, and real-world applications. The conference covers the following technical domains:

  • Digital intelligence systems, architectures, and frameworks
  • Generative AI, multimodal models, and foundational models
  • Machine learning algorithms, optimization techniques, and computational methods
  • Edge AI and distributed intelligence computing
  • Human–AI interaction, inclusion, and collaboration systems
  • Responsible AI, governance, ethics, policy, and societal implications
  • Engineering pipelines including MLOps, deployment and automation frameworks
  • Applications across sectors including healthcare, industry, government, defense, education, finance, cybersecurity, sustainability, agriculture, and creative technologies

    Research submissions may include theoretical models, empirical research, case studies, prototypes, simulation studies, industrial implementation frameworks, and early-stage conceptual manuscripts.

Call for Papers

EDGE 2026 invites original, unpublished research papers. All submissions will undergo a rigorous peer-review process following IEEE standards.

Accepted and presented papers will be submitted for inclusion in IEEE Xplore,subject to approval and compliance.

All conferences and conference proceedings must meet IEEEs quality standards, and IEEE reserves the right not to publish any proceedings that do not meet these standards. Indexing & Abstracting-The Conference Publication Program (CPP) assures wide distribution of conference proceedings by providing abstracting and indexing of all individual conference papers for inclusion in worldwide databases. The CPP makes every reasonable attempt to ensure that abstracts and index entries of content accepted into the program are included in databases provided by independent abstracting and indexing (A&I) services. Each A&I partner makes its own editorial decision on what content it will index. IEEE cannot guarantee entries are included in any particular database

Paper Submission

  • Papers must be original and not simultaneously submitted to another journal or conference.
  • Submissions should follow the IEEE conference template. Visit the link: https://www.ieee.org/conferences/publishing/templates.html
  • Papers should not exceed 6 pages including figures, tables, and references.
  • All submissions will be peer-reviewed and evaluated based on originality, technical quality, and relevance to the conference themes.
  • The working language of the conference is English, which will be used for all printed material, presentations and discussion.
  • Paper submission should be through CMT submission
  • Author must create a CMT account to submit their paper using the link: https://cmt3.research.microsoft.com/docs/help/general/account-creation.html
